What is environmental conservation and why is it important?

Environmental conservation entails safeguarding the natural world to avert its deterioration due to human activities like unsustainable agriculture, deforestation, and fossil fuel combustion.

The repercussions of these actions encompass polluted air, plastic contamination, habitat destruction, and, most critically, climate change. Scientists caution that delayed action could exacerbate natural calamities, elevate sea levels, intensify extreme weather events, leading to ecosystem collapse, widespread wildlife extinction, food shortages, and global displacement of populations.

As a collective of eco-conscious travelers, we cherish the wonders of the natural world and seek to engage with it in a conscientious, sustainable, and purposeful manner. At GBP, our internal operations are fully carbon neutral, and we've established a Climate Action Plan aimed at offsetting all volunteer flight emissions by 2030.

How can we save Earth?

There are many ways to save our planet including conserving water, reducing our use of oil and embracing green energy, reducing waste and single-use plastics and planting more trees. If you’re looking for small ways that you can make a difference, search online for ideas of how to get started.

Why is it important to save the Earth?

It is important to save Earth because so far, it is the only completely habitable planet that offers fresh water, food and oxygen for thousands of species, including humans.

What will happen to the Earth if we keep polluting?

More pollution will cause the Earth’s temperate to rise, causing a variety of issues including increased natural disasters, the destruction of habitats, and many other serious problems.
